Background

Introduced in 1945, MUN has been an avenue for young leaders to engage in the building blocks of global change. The art of consensus building, diplomatic negotiation, formal and informal debate and policy formation.

AccraWHO as a Model World Health Organisation conference

As one of the organs of the United Nations, the World Health Organization (WHO) is frequently tasked with finding solutions to health problems that pose global threats. The choice to simulate the World Health Assembly affords attendees the chance to build their understanding of medicine and public health policy, as well as increase their skills in international development and diplomacy.

Format of conferences

Participants come prepared to represent countries, news agencies or non-governmental organizations and while staying true to that role, debate and negotiate legislation which will be voted on by the entire committee. The simulation stays as true to correct UN procedure as possible, not only for the immersive effect, but also to keep proceedings running smoothly. The end goal is to produce a document of legislation, known as a resolution, which will be adopted by the committee and presented at the World Health Assembly for their consideration.

Target Audience

The 2020 edition is open to all students in the tertiary level irrespective of their faculty or field of study.
